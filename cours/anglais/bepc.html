INTRODUCTION GÉNÉRALE : L'ÉPREUVE D'ANGLAIS AU BEPC BURKINA FASO

Présentation de l'épreuve

L'épreuve d'anglais au BEPC dure 2 heures et est notée sur 100 points. Elle vise à évaluer les compétences de compréhension et d'expression écrites, ainsi que la maà®trise des structures grammaticales et du vocabulaire de base. Le coefficient est généralement 2.

L'épreuve se compose habituellement de :

  • Reading Comprehension (Compréhension de texte) : 20 à 30 points
  • Grammar and Vocabulary (Grammaire et vocabulaire) : 30 à 40 points
  • Writing (Expression écrite) : 30 à 40 points

Les thèmes abordés sont souvent liés à la vie quotidienne, la santé, l'environnement, la culture africaine et les réalités du Burkina Faso.

Conseil : bien lire les consignes, gérer son temps (30 min compréhension, 40 min grammaire, 50 min expression écrite), et utiliser un brouillon pour la rédaction.

PARTIE 1 : GRAMMAR REVIEW (RÉVISION GRAMMATICALE)

1. Les temps de l'indicatif (Tenses)

1.1 Present Simple / Present Continuous

Present Simple : habitude, vérité générale. I go to school every day. Water boils at 100°C.

Forme affirmative : sujet + base verbale (+ -s/-es à la 3ème personne du singulier). Négation : do/does + not + base verbale. Interrogation : Do/Does + sujet + base verbale ?

Present Continuous (be + V-ing) : action en cours, futur proche. She is reading now. We are travelling to Ouagadougou tomorrow.

Attention : certains verbes (like, love, know, want) ne s'emploient généralement pas au continuous.

1.2 Simple Past / Past Continuous

Simple Past : action terminée dans le passé. He visited his grandparents last week. Forme : verbes réguliers (V-ed), irréguliers (2ème colonne). Négation : did not + base verbale.

Past Continuous (was/were + V-ing) : action en cours dans le passé, souvent interrompue. I was doing my homework when the phone rang.

1.3 Present Perfect / Past Perfect

Present Perfect (have/has + past participle) : bilan présent, expérience, action passée avec conséquence présente. She has lived in Bobo-Dioulasso since 2019. Have you ever eaten mangoes?

Past Perfect (had + past participle) : action antérieure à une autre dans le passé. When I arrived, he had already left.

1.4 Future

  • Will + base verbale : décision spontanée, prédiction. I will help you.
  • Be going to + base verbale : intention, projet. We are going to study English next year.
  • Present Continuous : action planifiée. He is leaving for Koudougou tonight.

2. Les modaux (Modal Verbs)

Les modaux sont invariables (pas de -s à la 3ème personne) et suivis de la base verbale sans "to".

  • Can / Could : capacité, permission. I can swim. Could I borrow your pen?
  • Must / Have to : obligation. Students must wear uniforms. I have to finish my homework.
  • Should : conseil. You should drink more water during the dry season.
  • May / Might : possibilité. It may rain this evening. She might come later.
  • Would : conditionnel, politesse. I would like some water. Would you help me?

3. Comparatifs et superlatifs

  • Comparatif de supériorité : adjectif court + -er + than / more + adjectif long + than. He is taller than his brother. This book is more interesting than that one.
  • Superlatif : the + adjectif court + -est / the most + adjectif long. Mount Kilimanjaro is the highest mountain in Africa. She is the most intelligent student.
  • Formes irrégulières : good → better → the best ; bad → worse → the worst ; far → farther/further → the farthest/furthest.

Points clés / Astuces BEPC

  • Toujours repérer les indicateurs de temps (yesterday, tomorrow, since, last week…) pour choisir le bon temps.
  • Après un modal, jamais de "to", sauf "have to" et "ought to".
  • Attention aux 3èmes personnes du singulier au présent simple (don't forget the -s!).

Grammar Exercises

Exercise 1: Present Simple or Continuous?

Instructions : Complete the sentences with the correct form of the verb in brackets.

1. My sister usually (get up) …………… at 6 a.m.
2. Look! The children (play) …………… in the yard.
3. Water (freeze) …………… at 0°C.
4. I (not understand) …………… this exercise.

Correction

1. gets up (present simple, habit) | 2. are playing (present continuous, action en cours) | 3. freezes (vérité générale) | 4. do not understand (verbe d'état, pas de continuous)

Exercise 2: Past Simple or Present Perfect?

Instructions : Fill in the blanks with the correct tense.

1. She …………… (visit) her uncle last weekend.
2. I …………… (never/eat) fufu before.
3. We …………… (not/see) the new film yet.
4. When …………… you …………… (arrive) in Burkina Faso?

Correction

1. visited (past simple, last weekend) | 2. have never eaten (present perfect, expérience) | 3. haven't seen (present perfect + yet) | 4. did … arrive (past simple, question sur un moment précis)

Exercise 3: Modal Verbs

Instructions : Complete with can, must, should, may (positive or negative).

1. You …………… smoke here; it's forbidden.
2. …………… I open the window? It's very hot.
3. Students …………… wear their school uniform.
4. You …………… drink enough water during the hot season.

Correction

1. mustn't (interdiction) | 2. May/Can (permission) | 3. must (obligation) | 4. should (conseil)

Exercise 4: Comparatives and Superlatives

Instructions : Use the correct form of the adjective.

1. My brother is …………… (tall) than me.
2. This is the …………… (good) restaurant in town.
3. English is …………… (interesting) than mathematics, I think.
4. She is the …………… (intelligent) student in our class.

Correction

1. taller | 2. best | 3. more interesting | 4. most intelligent

Exercise 5: Prepositions

Instructions : Fill in with in, on, at, to, from.

1. I live …………… Ouagadougou.
2. My birthday is …………… March 15th.
3. She goes …………… school by bike.
4. We will meet …………… 8 o'clock.

Correction

1. in (city) | 2. on (date) | 3. to (direction) | 4. at (time)

PARTIE 2 : READING COMPREHENSION (COMPRÉHENSION DE TEXTE)

Méthodologie

  1. Lire le texte une première fois pour comprendre le sens général.
  2. Lire les questions avant une deuxième lecture plus attentive.
  3. Repérer les mots-clés dans les questions et dans le texte.
  4. Répondre avec des phrases complètes en anglais, en citant des éléments du texte si nécessaire.
  5. Ne pas recopier de longs passages sans les intégrer à votre réponse.

Types de questions : What is the text about? / True or False? Justify. / Find synonyms/antonyms in the text. / Answer the following questions. / What does the underlined word refer to?

Exemple de texte et questions

Text:

Rahima is a 15-year-old girl who lives in a small village near Bobo-Dioulasso. Every morning, she wakes up at 5:30 and helps her mother fetch water from the well, which is two kilometres away. After that, she goes to school on foot. She loves English and wants to become a teacher. Her father, who is a farmer, is very proud of her. However, during the rainy season, the roads become muddy and she sometimes arrives late. Despite these difficulties, Rahima works hard because she believes that education is the key to a better future.

Exercise 6: Questions on the text

1. How old is Rahima?
2. What does she do before going to school?
3. Why does she sometimes arrive late at school?
4. Find a synonym for "difficulties" in the text.
5. What does "she" (line 4) refer to?

Correction

1. She is 15 years old. | 2. She helps her mother fetch water from the well. | 3. Because the roads become muddy during the rainy season. | 4. "Difficulties" is not repeated, but "these difficulties" refers to the situation; a synonym could be "problems". (No direct synonym in the text) | 5. "She" refers to Rahima.

PARTIE 3 : WRITING (EXPRESSION ÉCRITE)

1. Écrire une lettre personnelle

Une lettre amicale (à un ami, à la famille) suit généralement cette structure :

  • Votre adresse (en haut à droite) : P.O. Box 123, Ouagadougou, Burkina Faso
  • Date : July 12, 2026
  • Salutation : Dear … ,
  • Corps : paragraphes avec une introduction (why you are writing), un développement (news, questions) et une conclusion (take care, write soon).
  • Formule de politesse : Best regards, / Yours, / Love,
  • Signature (prénom)

2. Écrire un paragraphe argumentatif

Un petit essai (10-15 lignes) doit contenir :

  • Topic sentence (phrase qui annonce l'idée principale).
  • Supporting sentences (arguments, exemples).
  • Concluding sentence (résumé ou ouverture).

Connecteurs utiles : First, secondly, moreover, however, for example, in conclusion.

3. Sujets types

  • Describe your best friend.
  • What are the advantages and disadvantages of living in a city?
  • Write a letter to your pen pal telling him/her about your last holidays.
  • Do you think that children should help with housework? Give your reasons.

Exercise 8: Write a paragraph

Instructions : Do you think that sport is important for young people? Write a paragraph of about 10 lines giving your opinion.

Correction possible

In my opinion, sport is very important for young people. First, it helps them stay healthy and strong. For example, running or playing football improves their physical condition. Secondly, sport teaches young people discipline and teamwork. When they play in a team, they learn to respect rules and cooperate with others. Moreover, sport is a good way to relax after studying and to make new friends. For all these reasons, I believe that every young person should practise a sport regularly.

PARTIE 4 : VOCABULARY (VOCABULAIRE THÉMATIQUE)

1. La famille (Family)

  • father, mother, brother, sister, uncle, aunt, cousin, nephew, niece, grandfather, grandmother, parents, children, son, daughter, husband, wife, relative.

2. L'école (School)

  • teacher, student, classroom, blackboard, desk, book, pen, pencil, exercise book, homework, lesson, subject, break, examination, diploma, to learn, to teach, to pass, to fail.

3. La santé (Health)

  • healthy, sick, ill, disease, fever, headache, stomach ache, medicine, hospital, doctor, nurse, patient, vaccination, prevention, hygiene, malaria, HIV/AIDS, mosquito net, to wash hands, to drink clean water.

4. L'environnement (Environment)

  • tree, forest, river, desert, drought, rain, pollution, waste, to protect, to plant, to recycle, nature, wildlife, climate change, deforestation.

5. Les métiers et professions (Jobs and occupations)

  • farmer, teacher, doctor, nurse, engineer, driver, tailor, carpenter, shopkeeper, policeman, soldier, civil servant, worker, journalist, musician.

6. Les loisirs et activités quotidiennes

  • to read, to listen to music, to watch TV, to play football, to dance, to cook, to fetch water, to sweep, to wash clothes, to go to the market, to visit friends, to rest.

7. Quelques adjectifs utiles

  • big/small, tall/short, happy/sad, rich/poor, beautiful/ugly, strong/weak, easy/difficult, old/young, new/old, clean/dirty, hot/cold, dry/wet, hungry/thirsty.

8. Faux amis (False friends) à connaà®tre

  • actually = en réalité (≠ actuellement = currently)
  • library = bibliothèque (≠ librairie = bookshop)
  • sensible = raisonnable (≠ sensible = sensitive)
  • sympathetic = compatissant (≠ sympathique = nice, friendly)
  • journey = voyage (≠ journée = day)

CONSEILS GLOBAUX POUR L'ÉPREUVE D'ANGLAIS

  • Bien lire toutes les consignes avant de commencer.
  • Pour la compréhension, chercher le sens général avant de répondre aux questions de détail.
  • Pour l'expression écrite, toujours faire un petit plan au brouillon.
  • Relire ses réponses pour éviter les fautes d'inattention (oublis de -s, accords).
  • Utiliser un vocabulaire simple mais correct : mieux vaut une phrase simple sans erreur qu'une phrase compliquée pleine de fautes.
  • Apprendre régulièrement du vocabulaire thématique et quelques expressions de liaison.
