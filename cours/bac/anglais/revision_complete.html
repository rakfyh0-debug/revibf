PARTIE 1 – Conjugaison et temps verbaux

1. Présent simple (Simple Present)

Formation : Base verbale (s à la 3e personne du singulier).
Emploi : Habitudes, vérités générales, actions régulières.
Ex : She works in a bank. Water boils at 100°C.

Adverbes associés : always, usually, often, sometimes, never, every day.

2. Présent continu (Present Continuous)

Formation : am/is/are + base verbale + -ing.
Emploi : Action en cours au moment présent, futur proche.
Ex : I am reading a book right now. She is leaving tomorrow.

3. Prétérit simple (Simple Past)

Formation : Verbe régulier : base + -ed ; verbes irréguliers : 2e colonne.
Emploi : Action terminée dans le passé, datée.
Ex : They visited Paris last year. He went to the market.

4. Prétérit continu (Past Continuous)

Formation : was/were + base verbale + -ing.
Emploi : Action en cours dans le passé, souvent interrompue par une autre.
Ex : I was sleeping when the phone rang.

5. Present perfect simple

Formation : have/has + participe passé (3e colonne).
Emploi : Action passée ayant un lien avec le présent (expérience, résultat, durée).
Ex : She has lived in London since 2010. I have never eaten sushi.

6. Present perfect continu (Present Perfect Continuous)

Formation : have/has + been + base verbale + -ing.
Emploi : Action commencée dans le passé et qui continue, ou qui vient de s'achever avec des conséquences visibles.
Ex : They have been waiting for hours. It has been raining all day.

7. Past perfect (Pluperfect)

Formation : had + participe passé.
Emploi : Action antérieure à une autre action passée.
Ex : When I arrived, the meeting had already started.

8. Futur (Future)

Will : décision spontanée, prédiction, promesse. I will help you.
Going to : intention, projet, prédiction basée sur des indices. Look at those clouds! It is going to rain.
Présent continu : projet déjà organisé. I am meeting her at 5.
Présent simple : horaire fixe. The train leaves at 6 p.m.

9. Conditionnel (Conditional)

Présent : would + base verbale. I would travel more if I had money.
Passé : would have + participe passé. She would have passed if she had studied.

10. Impératif (Imperative)

Base verbale (sans sujet). Listen carefully. Don't be late.

11. Gérondif et infinitif

Gérondif (base + -ing) : après certaines verbes (enjoy, avoid, suggest), prépositions, sujet de phrase. I enjoy reading.
Infinitif avec to : après want, decide, hope, etc. She wants to leave.
Infinitif sans to : après modaux, let, make. Let me go.

PARTIE 3 – Grammaire approfondie

A. Articles

A / an : article indéfini (singulier, général). a book, an apple.
The : article défini (objet spécifique ou connu). the sun, the book on the table.
Ø (zéro) : noms indénombrables ou pluriels génériques. Water is essential. Teachers are important.

B. Noms dénombrables et indénombrables

Dénombrables : peuvent être comptés (a chair, two chairs). Indénombrables : masse (rice, information, advice). Utiliser some, any, much, a lot of. There is some milk.

C. Adjectifs et adverbes

Adjectif : qualifie un nom. a beautiful dress.
Adverbe : modifie un verbe, un adjectif ou un autre adverbe (souvent -ly). She sings beautifully. He drives fast.

D. Comparatifs et superlatifs

Comparatif d'égalité : as + adj + as. She is as tall as her brother.
Comparatif de supériorité : adj court + -er + than / more + adj long + than. He is taller than me. This is more interesting.
Superlatif : the + adj court + -est / the most + adj long. the tallest, the most beautiful.
Irréguliers : good - better - best, bad - worse - worst, far - farther/further - farthest/furthest.

E. Prépositions

Temps : at (heures, fêtes), on (jours, dates), in (mois, années, saisons). at 5pm, on Monday, in July.
Lieu : at (point), on (surface), in (espace fermé). at the station, on the table, in the box.
Mouvement : to, into, onto, through, across, along.

F. Modaux (Modal verbs)

Can / could : capacité, permission. I can swim. Could I open the window?
Must / have to : obligation. You must stop. I have to go.
Mustn't : interdiction. You mustn't smoke here.
May / might : probabilité, permission polie. It may rain. May I come in?
Should / ought to : conseil. You should see a doctor.
Need : besoin (surtout à la forme négative). You needn't worry.

G. Voix passive (déjà traitée, rappel)

Formation : be + participe passé. The letter was sent yesterday.

H. Discours indirect (déjà traité)

Rappel des changements de temps et pronoms.

I. Conditionnelles (If-clauses) – rappel des types 0,1,2,3

Voir tableau précédent.

J. Subjonctif et souhaits

Wish + past simple : regret présent. I wish I had a car.
Wish + past perfect : regret passé. I wish I had studied.
It's time + past simple : suggestion. It's time we left.

K. Question tags

Reprise de l'auxiliaire, opposé. You are happy, aren't you? He doesn't like fish, does he?

L. Pronoms relatifs

Who : personne sujet. Whom : personne objet. Which : chose. That : les deux. Whose : possession. The man who called you. The book that I read.

PARTIE 4 – Vocabulaire thématique

A. Famille et relations (Family and relationships)

  • parents, father, mother, sibling, brother, sister, uncle, aunt, cousin, nephew, niece, grandparents, grandson.
  • marriage, wedding, divorce, single, engaged, relative, in-laws.

B. École et éducation (School and education)

  • classroom, teacher, student, pupil, headmaster, subject, timetable, homework, exam, grade, diploma, degree, scholarship.
  • to learn, to teach, to pass, to fail, to graduate, to attend.

C. Travail et professions (Jobs and work)

  • doctor, nurse, engineer, lawyer, teacher, farmer, trader, civil servant, employee, employer.
  • salary, wage, contract, unemployment, retirement, to apply for a job, CV, interview.

D. Santé et corps humain (Health and body)

  • head, arm, leg, heart, stomach, back, skin, blood.
  • headache, fever, cough, injury, disease, treatment, medicine, pharmacy.

E. Nourriture et boissons (Food and drinks)

  • rice, bread, meat, fish, vegetable, fruit, milk, water, oil, sugar.
  • breakfast, lunch, dinner, meal, to cook, to boil, to fry, to roast.

F. Logement (Housing)

  • house, flat, room, kitchen, bedroom, bathroom, living room, garden, roof, wall.
  • furniture: chair, table, bed, sofa, cupboard.

G. Transports et voyages (Transport and travel)

  • car, bus, train, plane, bicycle, motorcycle, boat.
  • ticket, journey, trip, luggage, passport, to travel, to drive, to fly.

H. Environnement et nature (Environment and nature)

  • climate, weather, rain, drought, forest, desert, river, mountain, animal, plant.
  • pollution, waste, to protect, to conserve, renewable energy.

I. Technologies et communication

  • computer, internet, smartphone, social media, email, website, to download, to upload, password, screen.

J. Culture et loisirs

  • music, film, book, sport, football, game, art, tradition, festival, museum.
  • to sing, to dance, to play, to read, to draw.
